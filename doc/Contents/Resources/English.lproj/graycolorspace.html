Gray

Converting a color to gray can be done using the channels of different colorspaces. The following options can be selected for computing the gray value:

  • L (HSL): The L channel of the HSL space is used as the gray value.
  • V (HSV): The V channel of the HSV space is used as the gray value.
  • G (RGB): The G channel of the RGB space is used as the gray value.
  • L* (CIELAB, Luv): The L* response is used as a gray value. Note that this setting will always use the precise L* response, no matter what response is set at the Lab colorspace.
  • L (Lab, Lch): The L channel of the Lab or Lch space is used as the gray value. Note that the gray value therefore corresponds to the response setting of the Lab colorspace.
  • Y (XYZ, Yxy, Yuv): The Y channel of the XYZ, Yxy or Yuv space is used as the gray value.
  • Y' (YCbCr): The Y' channel of the YCbCr space is used as the gray value.

As a default, the L* value is chosen.
